CLIMATE CHANGE

Who's sowing, and who's reaping

Climate change is an ever present worry for us all, but the reality is that the inevitable repurcussions of decades of pollution will disproportionately affect developing countries: those who contributed very little to the problem we as a planet currently face, and do often not have the necessary infrastructure to manage the problem. Here we see CO2 emissions per capita of all countries since 1990. The predictable trend we see is that richer, more developed countries contributing at much higher rates than developing countries.

What we see in the CO2 contribution chart above is almost opposite to what we see in those countries most affected by that same pollution.
